Would you translate this as "In a train to Tirol"?

I have seen this sign many times and always admired the excellent wordplay. As far as I know there is no English version, and I don't think there needs to be. I think it is mainly aimed at Germans driving through a part of Tirol in Austria. But it is a great exercise. "In a train to Tirol" would be an obvious translation that a normal translator would be tempted to use. But it would lose that wonderful wordplay and turn a very clever strapline into something completely flat.

But what could we use instead?
